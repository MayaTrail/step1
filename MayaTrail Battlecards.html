Mitigant closest direct competitor

"Cloud-native Adversarial Exposure Validation. Validate your detections continuously."

THREAT LEVEL HIGH
THEIR PITCH

Continuous Adversarial Exposure Validation across AWS, Azure, and (planned) GCP. BYOR (bring-your-own-role) safety model, no long-lived keys. Attack Builder UI for composing custom chains. AI-suggested next emulation based on your last run's gaps. Microsoft Sentinel integration with detection content flowing into your SIEM.

They sell "validate continuously" as the value frame.

WHEN THEY WIN
  • Multi-cloud buyer. Heavy Azure or GCP presence, needs day-one parity.
  • SOC operations buyer. Wants a dashboard and integration with their existing SIEM, not a CLI output.
  • Enterprise with vendor security questionnaire fatigue. Mitigant is Series A; closed-source; predictable.
  • "Continuous validation" budget line. Some companies have explicit CTEM/AEV budget categories Mitigant fits cleanly.
WHEN WE WIN
  • Detection-engineering buyer. Wants to own the rules, not subscribe to them. We ship rules as commits.
  • AWS-first depth needed. Buyer cares about AMBERSQUID-specific or Scattered-Spider-specific drills, not generic "lateral movement".
  • OSS sophistication. Engineering-led teams that vet by reading source.
  • Portable evidence required. Audit, compliance, or air-gap needs that a SaaS dashboard can't serve.
  • Price-conscious buyer. Our Team tier is below Mitigant's enterprise floor.
DO NOT
  • Trash-talk Mitigant in a call. Their product is good; the buyer can tell when you're punching down.
  • Pretend to compete on multi-cloud. We don't, and pretending hurts trust.
  • Out-feature them on dashboards. Different product. Don't drag the conversation into UI comparisons.
OBJECTION
"Mitigant is multi-cloud and we need Azure too."
RESPONSE
"Be honest. If you need Azure parity today, Mitigant is the right pick. We're AWS-first and we will not pretend otherwise. If you're >50% AWS and want the deepest catalogue + portable rules, we're a better fit. Should I introduce you to a customer running both?"
OBJECTION
"Mitigant does continuous validation - yours is one-shot drills."
RESPONSE
"Different by design. Continuous validation is great for the dashboard-buyer. We're built for detection engineers who ship rules - the drill ends when the rule is in your repo. We integrate with your CI/CD, not your SIEM."

Stratus Red Team datadog OSS · adjacent

"Granular, actionable adversary emulation for the cloud."

THREAT LEVEL MEDIUM
THEIR PITCH

Open-source Go CLI by Christophe Tafani-Dereeper (DataDog). Atomic technique testing for AWS, Azure, GCP, Kubernetes. Tagged with MITRE ATT&CK. ~50 techniques, each runnable with one command, idempotent, with cleanup. Mature OSS, well-engineered, well-loved by detection engineers.

WHEN THEY WIN
  • Unit-testing-style detection work. "Did my rule fire on a single CreateAccessKey call?"
  • Buyer wants free, well-maintained OSS, no commercial conversation.
  • DataDog SIEM customer. Tight integration loop with the rest of their stack.
  • Multi-cloud parity on day one (they ship AWS + Azure + GCP).
WHEN WE WIN
  • Buyer wants APT chains, not atomic tests. Stratus runs one technique; we chain 8-15 across services.
  • Buyer wants detection content shipped with the run. Stratus produces stdout/logs only; we ship Sigma rules as artifacts.
  • Audit/compliance evidence needed. Stratus has no signer or evidence bundle.
  • Buyer wants hosted workspace + adapters. Stratus is CLI-only.
  • Buyer wants APT-named editorial. Stratus is technique-named; we are APT-named.
POSITIONING

Complementary, not competing. A sophisticated buyer often uses both. Stratus for unit testing during rule authoring, MayaTrail for chain-level drills + evidence. We can credibly say: "Use Stratus for atomic tests; use us when you need the chain."

OBJECTION
"We already use Stratus Red Team. Why MayaTrail?"
RESPONSE
"Great choice for atomic tests. Stratus runs a single technique end-to-end. When your team needs to test whether a 13-step APT chain fires - AMBERSQUID, Scattered Spider - that's where we slot in. Some teams use both: Stratus for unit testing, MayaTrail for chain-level drills."
OBJECTION
"Why pay if Stratus is free and works?"
RESPONSE
"Don't pay if it works. Our OSS engine + 6 APT chains are free too. The hosted tier is for teams that need the signer, the SIEM adapters, custom chains, or a shared workspace. If Stratus is solving your problem, keep using it."

CloudGoat vulnerable AWS lab

"Rhino Security Labs' 'Vulnerable by Design' AWS deployment tool."

THREAT LEVEL LOW
THEIR PITCH

Spin up an intentionally vulnerable AWS environment for hands-on pentest training. 10+ scenarios from IAM priv-esc to RCE-on-S3-via-Lambda. Deployed via terraform, designed to be broken into and torn down. CTF flavor.

WHEN THEY WIN
  • Security training / education. Bootcamps, university courses, individual learners.
  • Red team practice. Internal red teams want a target to break into.
  • Free pentest lab need. Buyer wants disposable, intentionally-broken infra.
WHEN WE WIN
  • Detection-engineering buyer. CloudGoat exists to be hacked; MayaTrail exists to test detections. Different deliverable, different buyer.
  • Output matters. CloudGoat gives you a CTF flag; we give you Sigma rules.
  • Scale. CloudGoat is for one engineer to break; we run drills for full detection teams.
  • Audit/compliance evidence. CloudGoat has none.
POSITIONING

Different category. CloudGoat = vulnerable lab to attack. MayaTrail = attack chain run against a sandbox the tool provisions, with detection content as output. "CloudGoat trains attackers; we equip defenders."

OBJECTION
"Isn't this just CloudGoat with more steps?"
RESPONSE
"Different goal. CloudGoat is a target you break into - useful for training. We're a drill that grades your existing detection content. We ship the Sigma rules; CloudGoat ships flags. Both have a place; they don't compete."

Pacu aws pentest framework

"The first true open-source AWS exploitation framework."

THREAT LEVEL LOW
THEIR PITCH

The Metasploit of AWS. Operator-driven CLI framework with 30+ "modules" - IAM enumeration, privilege escalation, evasion. Designed for offensive operators conducting cloud assessments. Mature, respected in the pentest community.

WHEN THEY WIN
  • Pentest team / red team buyer. Wants an offensive toolkit, not a defensive product.
  • Consulting engagements. Pacu is the standard tool for AWS engagements.
  • Long-lived key scenario. Some Pacu modules expect long-lived IAM credentials (where ours uses STS).
WHEN WE WIN
  • Detection engineer buyer. Pacu is operator-driven; we're scripted-and-replayable.
  • Reproducibility needed. Pacu runs depend on the human; ours are byte-identical run-to-run.
  • Evidence package. Pacu produces session logs; we produce hash-chained bundles.
  • Detection content output. Pacu doesn't ship Sigma rules.
  • Safety transparency. We're STS-scoped + Pulumi destroy; Pacu uses long-lived keys.
POSITIONING

Different buyer entirely. Pacu sells to pentesters; we sell to detection engineers. "Pacu is offensive; we're a drill for defenders." Don't confuse them in conversation - they serve completely different workflows.

OBJECTION
"We have a red team using Pacu - why a second tool?"
RESPONSE
"Your red team should keep Pacu. They're solving a different problem - manual exploitation. We're for your detection engineers to run scripted, repeatable drills. Different teams, different workflows, complementary. Many shops run both."

Atomic Red Team red canary OSS · host-focused

"Library of simple, focused tests aligned with MITRE ATT&CK."

THREAT LEVEL LOW
THEIR PITCH

The biggest open library of atomic technique tests, organized by MITRE ATT&CK. ~1,500 tests across Windows, Linux, macOS, with some cloud coverage added later. Each test is a YAML file + script, easy to run, easy to extend. The de-facto standard for on-host atomic emulation.

WHEN THEY WIN
  • Endpoint-detection buyer. Their bread-and-butter is host-level technique testing.
  • Atomic-test workflow already in place. Many SOCs have CI pipelines around Atomic.
  • Free, free, free. Massive library, zero cost, no upgrade pressure.
WHEN WE WIN
  • Cloud-shaped attack. Atomic is on-host; cloud APT chains live on the control plane.
  • APT-named chain requested. Atomic is technique-named; we're APT-named.
  • Detection content as output. Atomic doesn't ship Sigma rules with tests.
  • Signed evidence bundle. Atomic is logs-only.
  • Cloud-native team that doesn't want host-shaped tooling.
POSITIONING

Different category - host vs cloud. Atomic covers what runs on a VM; we cover what runs against the AWS API. "Atomic Red Team for endpoints; MayaTrail for cloud control plane." Don't pretend to replace Atomic; we complement it.

OBJECTION
"We use Atomic Red Team. Isn't this the same thing?"
RESPONSE
"Different problem. Atomic tests what happens on a host - processes, files, registry. We test what happens at the AWS API layer - CreateRole, AssumeRole, StopLogging. Cloud APTs don't show up on hosts; they show up in CloudTrail. We're the cloud equivalent."

UNIVERSAL OBJECTIONS USE WITH ANY COMPETITOR

Lines that work everywhere.

"WE'LL JUST BUILD IT IN-HOUSE"
"You can - many do. The math: a senior detection engineer takes 2-3 days per APT chain to research, write, and validate. You'd be in-house six months before you have what we ship Tuesday. Want to see the AMBERSQUID chain?"
"WE NEED MULTI-CLOUD"
"Honest answer: we're AWS-first. Azure-light is on the 2027 roadmap. If you're >50% AWS, we're a great fit today. If you need Azure parity now, we'll point you to Mitigant and stay in touch for when AWS depth matters."
"IS THIS SAFE FOR PRODUCTION?"
"It never touches production. Sandbox account, STS-only role, Pulumi sandbox spun up per run, one-command destroy with post-condition. We'll walk you through the safety model in 5 minutes."
"WHAT'S YOUR DIFFERENTIATION VS [ANYTHING]?"
"Three things. Named APTs not abstract scenarios. Detection rules shipped as evidence not as integration. Open core - the engine and every rule are free forever. Mitigant is the closest competitor and even they don't do all three."
"WHY OPEN-CORE?"
"Detection engineers buy from peers, not vendors. If they can read the engine, vet the chains, and run it without paying us a cent, they trust the product. The hosted tier is for teams that need workflow, signer, adapters - not features they could get free."
"WE ALREADY HAVE SOAR (TINES / TORQ / SPLUNK SOAR)"
"Different layer. SOAR executes the playbooks you've already written. We author the playbook from a real attack chain - 700+ lines per chain, with AWS CLI queries and containment steps. Drop our playbook into your SOAR and it has something to automate. Complementary, not competing."
