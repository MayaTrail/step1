CLOSEST COMPETITOR · MITIGANT

The direct comparison is Mitigant.

Berlin-based, SaaS, multi-cloud. Targets the same buyer (detection engineers + purple teams) and positions in the same category ("Cloud Attack Emulation" / "Adversarial Exposure Validation"). Their material is the clearest signal in the market for where this category is heading.

Mitigant · what they do well

BYOR - Bring Your Own Role

You grant a scoped role; they never hold long-lived keys. Sets the credibility bar for safety transparency. Same pattern as our STS-only model.

Attack Builder

UI to compose custom chains, not just pick from a catalog. Lets red teams model attacks specific to their environment instead of running canned tests.

AI recommendations

Suggests next emulation based on your last run's gaps. Good UX for continuous-validation framing.

Microsoft Sentinel integration

Detection content flows into the SIEM the customer already has. Tight integration loop with the detection team's stack.

Dynamic snapshotting

State capture + rollback during runs - makes "what changed" easier to audit and gives a clean undo if a chain partially executes.

MayaTrail · where we differentiate

APT-led catalogue, not abstract scenarios

Every chain traces to a named, observed campaign (SCATTERED SPIDER, AMBERSQUID, BlackCat, TeamTNT). Concrete, attributable, board-explainable - not "Persistence test #3".

Detection content ships with the run

Sigma + KQL rules + IR playbook are generated as evidence artifacts of the same emulation. Mitigant integrates detection-side; we author it.

Signed, hash-chained evidence package

Every run produces a verifiable bundle (CloudTrail JSON + MITRE map + report). Forensic-grade, auditable, replayable offline - not just a vendor dashboard.

OSS core (Apache 2.0)

Run the engine self-hosted with byte-identical output. The hosted tier adds the control plane and adapters, not the chains themselves.

Detection-engineering framing

The Run UI grades your stack: coverage %, MTTR, missed rules with PR-ready stubs. Outcomes flow into the detection repo, not back into the vendor.

INSPIRATIONS · WHAT TO TAKE · WHAT TO REFUSE

Borrow the safety language. Refuse the closed loop.

Direct lifts from Mitigant's playbook + things we should explicitly NOT do. Filed by category.

BORROW · 4 PATTERNS

Inspirations we should adopt.

  • Pre-run scope card. Show role ARN, blast radius, cost cap, rollback method before "Begin drill" - already in Run v2 plan view.
  • AI-suggested next emulation. Use this run's gaps to recommend the next APT - closes the continuous-validation loop.
  • Attack Builder for custom chains. Eventually let buyers compose their own (cherry-picked technique chains) - not just our 6 APTs.
  • Snapshot & rollback. If a chain partially executes, give a clean undo. Critical for trust during early beta.
REFUSE · 4 PATTERNS

Anti-patterns we should reject.

  • Closed detection content. Mitigant integrates with SIEMs; we author Sigma/KQL as evidence. Detection content is the product, not an integration.
  • Generic technique names. "Lateral Movement scenario" is abstract. AMBERSQUID, Scattered Spider, BlackCat - these names sell. Always APT-led.
  • Vendor-locked evidence. Refuse a UI-only dashboard. Every run must export a portable, hash-chained, offline-replayable bundle.
  • Continuous validation framing. Refuse "always-on testing." We're not BAS. We're a graded drill - punctuated, evidentiary, with receipts.

POSITIONING STATEMENT

Cloud attack emulation, audited by default.

Mitigant is selling adversarial exposure validation: continuous, hosted, integrated with your SIEM.

We sell graded drills with signed evidence: every run is a forensic event your detection team owns - the rules to ship, the playbook to file, the CloudTrail bundle to keep. Open core. APT-named. AWS-first.
