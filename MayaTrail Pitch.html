WHY THE GAP EXISTS

Most detection content was written for endpoints.
The attackers moved to control planes.

Sigma rules, SIEM content libraries, EDR signatures - the bulk of the detection-engineering corpus targets process and file behavior. But the modern cloud attack is a sequence of signed AWS API calls against a service mesh: AttachRolePolicy, AssumeRole, CreateApp, GetSecretValue, StopLogging. The control plane is where the kill chain runs - and where coverage is weakest.

HOW TEAMS TEST TODAY

Five workarounds. None of them prove cloud detections fire.

01 · TABLETOPS
Talk, don't test

SOC walks through a scenario verbally with the IR team. Quarterly. No actual rules exercised.

no signal on real detection content
02 · ATOMIC TESTS
Stratus / Atomic Red Team

One technique at a time, in isolation. Useful for unit-testing but a real APT chains 8-15 steps across services.

no signal on chained kill paths
03 · CSPM
Wiz, Prisma, Lacework

Find misconfigurations, posture drift, asset inventory. Excellent at config; not designed to test behavior.

posture not detection
04 · ANNUAL PENTEST
External engagement

Two weeks once a year. Skilled but episodic. Detection team rarely sees the chain in their own SIEM.

snapshot not continuous
05 · ENTERPRISE BAS
AttackIQ, SafeBreach

Agent-based, endpoint-shaped, six-figure contracts. Cloud control-plane chains are not the focus.

host-shaped, not cloud-shaped

Detection teams are flying blind on the place attacks actually land. We built MayaTrail because we were that team.

MayaTrail · the product 05 / 14
HOW IT WORKS

Four steps. No agents. No long-lived keys.

01 · CONNECT

STS role, two lines

Grant MayaTrail an assumable role in a scoped sandbox account. Trust policy is two lines. No credentials leave AWS.

02 · PROVISION

Pulumi sandbox

An intentionally-leaky stack spins up in ~60s. Tagged with run-id. Costs $0.003 / hour standing. Mirrors a real account in shape, not in data.

03 · RUN

Execute a named APT

Pick a chain. Watch it land in your CloudTrail. Every step narrated with MITRE technique and the Sigma rule that should fire.

04 · TEAR DOWN

One command, signed bundle

pulumi destroy with a post-condition: every resource must return NotFound. Evidence packet is yours to keep, signed.

A typical AMBERSQUID drill: ~12 minutes wall clock · 13 techniques · 11 Sigma rules generated · $1.40 worst-case AWS spend · clean teardown.

DEFENSIBILITY

01

APT-led catalogue with attribution

Every chain traces to a named, observed adversary. Building this requires offensive cloud-research depth + threat-intel attribution. We have both founders. OSS engine and chains are Apache 2.0 - hosted control plane adds the signer, run history, adapters. Adoption flywheel: every public run improves shared rules · every customer carries our signed bundle into their workflow. Same model that made Hashi/dbt defensible.
